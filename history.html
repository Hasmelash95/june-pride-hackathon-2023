LGBTQ+ Events and People

Welcome to our LGBTQ+ History page! Explore the rich tapestry of historical figures and events that have shaped the LGBTQ+ community. From trailblazing activists who fought for equal rights and acceptance to artists who expressed their identity through their craft, this collection celebrates the diverse voices and contributions of LGBTQ+ individuals throughout history. Delve into the stories of prominent figures like Bayard Rustin, Frida Kahlo, Marsha P. Johnson, and Alan Turing, and learn about the significant events that have paved the way for progress and equality. Join us on this journey of discovery as we honor the resilience, courage, and triumphs of LGBTQ+ people throughout the ages.

Ellen DeGeneres is an American comedian, actress, and talk show host. She came out as a lesbian in 1997 and has since been a prominent advocate for LGBTQ+ rights. Through her television show and various philanthropic efforts, DeGeneres has helped raise awareness and promote equality for the LGBTQ+ community

Marsha P. Johnson, an African-American transgender activist, played a pivotal role in the Stonewall uprising of 1969, a turning point in the LGBTQ+ rights movement. Co-founding the Street Transvestite Action Revolutionaries (STAR), Johnson tirelessly fought for the rights of transgender and homeless communities. Her legacy as a trailblazer, advocate, and symbol of resilience continues to inspire and uplift marginalized voices within the LGBTQ+ community.

RuPaul, whose full name is RuPaul Andre Charles, is an American drag queen, actor, and television personality. He is best known as the host and creator of the reality competition show "RuPaul's Drag Race." RuPaul has been a prominent figure in the LGBTQ+ community for decades and has used his platform to support and empower queer individuals.

Alan Turing, a brilliant British mathematician, computer scientist, and gay icon, broke the German Enigma code during WWII. His pioneering work laid the foundation for modern computing. Despite facing persecution for his sexuality, Turing's legacy continues to inspire, as he stands as a symbol of resilience, intellect, and the fight for LGBTQ+ rights.
